# **V. REGULATORY SHIFTS — LEGISLATION & POLICY TO WATCH**

**5.1  National Skills Development Authority (NSDA) 2.0 Reform (Impact: 9/10 | Short-Term)**

The NSDA is undergoing its most significant structural reform since establishment, with a proposed National Skills Authority Act expected to be tabled in Parliament in H2 2026\. Key provisions expected to include: mandatory accreditation for all private training institutions with \>200 annual enrolments; outcome-reporting requirements for placement rates and earnings growth; a National Qualifications Framework (NQF) expansion to Levels 1–8 (from current 1–5); and a BDT 1,200 Crore National Skills Fund for approved institutional grants. Early engagement with NSDA reform committee provides regulatory shaping opportunities.

**5.2  Smart Bangladesh Digital Skills Policy Framework (Impact: 8/10 | Mid-Term)**

Under Smart Bangladesh Vision 2041, the ICT Division is expected to formalise a Digital Skills Policy Framework by Q3 2026, mandating digital literacy standards for public sector employment, establishing a 'Digital Skills Passport' for formal job market access, and creating preferred procurement status for a2i-certified EdTech providers. Private operators who earn a2i-certification prior to the policy formalisation will lock in preferred provider status under subsequent public procurement.

**5.3  Bangladesh Medical & Dental Council — CME Mandatory Credits (Impact: 9/10 | Short-Term)**

BMDC has circulated a draft CME Policy (2025) mandating a minimum 30 CME credits per 3-year licensure cycle for all registered medical and dental practitioners (\~120,000 individuals), and 20 credits for AHPs. This creates an immediate, captive, recurring revenue market for BMDC-approved CME providers. Applications for BMDC CME provider accreditation open Q2 2026\. First-mover incumbency in this market is estimated to be worth BDT 60–120 Crore annually in recurring subscription revenues.

**5.4  Foreign Investment Facilitation Act Amendments — EdTech JV Facilitation (Impact: 7/10 | Mid-Term)**

BIDA's proposed Foreign Investment Facilitation Act amendments (expected FY2026–27) are designed to reduce foreign equity restrictions in EdTech from 49% to 74%, unlocking international strategic investors and MNCs (Pearson, Coursera, ETS) to enter Bangladesh via joint ventures. This is a double-edged signal: it accelerates inbound competition but also enables your company to attract foreign strategic investment at improved valuations.

**5.5  BTRC Spectrum & Digital Connectivity Policy (Impact: 7/10 | Mid-Term)**

Bangladesh Telecommunication Regulatory Commission's National Broadband Policy 2026–2030 targets 90% population coverage at minimum 10 Mbps by 2028, with 5G rollout in 8 divisional cities by Q4 2027\. This material improvement in connectivity infrastructure directly enables rural skilling expansion, live virtual classroom delivery, and AI-powered adaptive platforms — removing the last major infrastructure constraint on national-scale digital skilling.

## **12.3  Government & Regulatory Tailwinds for Freelancing**

The Bangladesh government has made freelancing a declared national economic priority under Smart Bangladesh Vision 2041\. The regulatory environment is strongly supportive:

* ICT Division Freelancing Income Incentive: 10% cash incentive on freelancing remittances channelled through formal banking \-- directly incentivising platform formalisation and financial literacy adoption

* 100% Tax Exemption on Freelancing Income: Extended through FY2024; renewal under discussion for 2025-2028 cycle in alignment with Smart Bangladesh Digital Economy targets

* Freelancer Skilling Programme (a2i): BDT 120 Crore+ allocated to structured digital skills training targeting 500,000 new freelancers by 2027 \-- preferred provider procurement open to NSDA-accredited institutions

* BASIS & ICT Division USD 5B IT/ITES Export Target by 2027: Requires approximately 3 million additional skilled digital workers, creating a structural, multi-year government procurement pipeline for qualified skilling providers

* PayPal Gap \-- Critical Unresolved Market Failure: PayPal remains unavailable in Bangladesh, forcing USD 500M+ in annual freelancing earnings through unofficial channels. Government in active negotiations (2025-26) to resolve. When resolved, this will unlock verifiable freelancer income data enabling income-share agreement (ISA) products \-- the next major skilling finance frontier in Bangladesh

*The PayPal resolution is a strategic inflection point. When formalised income flows become trackable, ISA-backed skilling products will become viable at scale \-- fundamentally changing the unit economics of the freelancing skilling market. Position your company to be ISA-ready before this happens.*
